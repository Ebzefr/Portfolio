Lofis Restaurant App

Ebenezer Obi
March 2024

This project is a UX/UI mobile app prototype designed for a modern restaurant, built using Figma. The goal was to create a seamless and inclusive experience for users to easily make reservations, order food, view dietary information, and track deliveries in real time.
The design focuses on accessibility, user diversity, and intuitive navigation. Key features include dietary filters (vegan, gluten-free, allergy-safe), a simplified booking process, and a live map for delivery tracking. Personas ranged from busy professionals to elderly users, ensuring the app meets a wide spectrum of user needs.

Problems

  • Users find the reservation process too time-consuming and confusing.
  • Dietary restrictions are hard to filter and view in the menu.
  • Users experience uncertainty about food delivery status.
  • Older users struggle with navigating the app interface.

Goals

  • Simplify the reservation flow with fewer steps and clear prompts.
  • Implement intuitive dietary filters and display clear ingredient/allergy info.
  • Integrate a real-time delivery tracker with live map and ETA updates.
  • Design an accessible, senior-friendly UI with larger touch targets and simple language.

Research

To design a user-centered restaurant app, I began with exploratory research to understand the needs, behaviors, and pain points of potential users. Pain Points

  • Difficult navigation for reservations.
  • Lack of detailed dietary information.
  • Confusing menu layout.
  • Unclear or slow delivery tracking.

Solution

  • Simplify the reservation process by providing a one-click reservation feature and a calendar view with availability.
  • Add a dietary filter that can display specific dietary needs.
  • Redesign the menu to be categorized by food types.
  • Implement a live map that shows the real-time location and time of the delivery.
