    Scenarios

    Scenario 1

    Alex

    For the upcoming weekend, Alex intended to arrange a unique and unexpected reservation at the restaurant for himself and his grandsons. Although Alex typically prefers to make reservations in person, he tried the digital approach for the first time by using the restaurant's mobile app on his smartphone. Even though he was familiar with the menu, he was unable to locate the "book a table" feature. Alex was stucked and asked his daughter for assistance. He mentioned how much easier it would be if the "book" button appeared immediately after logging in.

    Scenario 2

    Arnold

    Arnold is known for eating-out, after a busy day at work instead of worrying about what to cook he decides to have Chinese ribs and some drinks. Being shellfish allergic, he was dissatisfied to see that there was no obvious notice of the allergy content for each item when he first opened the app and looked through the menu. Concerned Arnold proceeds to the restaurant. He expressed his hope that the app would incorporate his feedback, which emphasised the value of having allergy information easily accessible.

    Scenario 3

    Lisa

    Lisa, the restaurant manager, observed a persistent problem with the online reservation system. It was difficult for customers to see the locations in real- time, and they frequently expressed irritation about the absent of this tool. In order to guarantee a smooth and enjoyable dining experience, Lisa has asked that a "location" feature be added to the mobile app.

    Understanding

    User Requirments

    • Simplified Navigation: A clear and intuitive way to make reservations without requiring assistance.
    • User-Friendly Design: A design that caters to users unfamiliar with digital platforms.
    • Allergy Information: Clear and visible labels or indicators for food items containing allergens like shellfish.
    • Real-Time Location Updates: A feature to display available tables or sections in real-time for better planning.
    • Operational Efficiency: Tools to streamline reservation management and reduce complaints.

    System Requirments

    • Prominent Call-to-Action (CTA): A "Book a Table" button that is clearly visible on the home page or immediately after login.
    • Accessibility Features: Ensure the app is easy to use for older generations or less tech-savvy users.
    • Allergen Information Database: Comprehensive and easily searchable allergy information for menu items.
    • Real-Time Table Availability: Integrate a real-time location feature that allows users to see available tables or areas in the restaurant.
